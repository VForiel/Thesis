% Abstract
\begin{abstract}
    The direct detection of exoplanets requires overcoming extreme contrast ratios between the star and its companion.
    This thesis presents the development of Tunable Kernel Nulling, a robust interferometric technique.
    We introduce theoretical models, the \phise{} simulation framework, and experimental results from the \phobos{} test bench.
\end{abstract}
